History of the Internet

10:43am - June 28, 2023

By definition, Web 2.0 is quite literally the second stage of development of the internet. Coined by Darcy DiNucci in 1999, it signified the transition from boring static web pages to more dynamic pages with user-generated content. This also meant the first rise of social media and the internet being used for person-to-person communication.

There is no definite definition for Web 2.0, and there is no strict start and end date for it either. Web 2.0, while having a visual impact was also a shift in the philosophy of web companies and developers. Whereas the internet was first used as a tool, it started to become a part of who we are instead. The web became a platform rather than just a network.

Web 2.0 allowed people to operate on real-time servers and communicate with others while posting their own content thanks to the help of the hypertext transfer protocol (HTTP). The introduction of application programming interfaces (API) allowed web developers to do more with their websites and communicate with their users and the applications. Technology that had arisen from this change in philosophy are JavaScript, Adobe Flash (rip), MS Silverlight, Eclipse, and Ajax.

Web 2.0 was able to garner more users of the internet, thanks to such technologies as Ajax which allowed websites to communicate with the browser automatically. Improvements to the usability of websites meant that more people would be willing to use them. This allowed the transition of the internet into the social web, focusing on the human interaction aspect of websites rather than a tool.
